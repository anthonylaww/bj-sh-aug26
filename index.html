Trip shape

at a glance

Mondays (3 Aug arrival, 9 Aug train day) are deliberately light — most major museums close Mondays, so that's when you rest, shop, or eat. Beijing alternates easy/active from Day 2. Shanghai mirrors the same rhythm: two shopping days to settle in off the train, then active/easy to the end.

time-sensitive

Before you go

must-read
✓ No visa needed

Entry — UK passports

UK ordinary passport holders enter China visa-free for up to 30 days (tourism) through 31 Dec 2026. Passport must be valid 6+ months beyond entry with 2 blank pages. Fill the free online arrival card before flying. If either of you holds a non-UK passport, check that nationality separately.

⚠ Set up before landing

Phone, payments & maps

  • eSIM with built-in VPN — Google, WhatsApp, Instagram all blocked without it
  • Alipay or WeChat Pay — link a UK card; this is how you pay everywhere including street stalls
  • Maps — Apple Maps (accurate in China) or Amap 高德; Google Maps pins are wrong
  • Dianping 大众点评 — China's Yelp; essential for food, queues, booking
  • Download offline translation pack; menus often aren't in English
⚠ Book 7 days out exactly

Forbidden City

Walk-up tickets are gone. Daily cap is 40,000 and sells out minutes after release. Tickets open at 20:00 Beijing time, exactly 7 days before your visit. Book at bookingticket.dpm.org.cn (English, passport number, no Chinese phone needed) or via Klook. Book Tiananmen Square separately (free but also requires reservation). Bring the physical passport you booked with — it's your entry scan. Closed Mondays.

🌡 August weather

Climate & packing

Beijing: 28–35°C, humid, afternoon thunderstorms possible. Shanghai: 30–38°C, very humid, typhoon season. Both cities: build in midday rests (13:00–15:00). Pack light breathable clothes, a compact umbrella, and sunscreen. Most indoor spaces are heavily air-conditioned — a light layer helps.

Getting around

  • Didi — the Chinese Uber. Works with Alipay. Essential for anything not on the metro.
  • Metro — both cities have excellent, cheap, English-signed metros. Buy on Alipay.
  • Bullet train BJS→SHA — Beijing South (南站) at 14:00, arrives Shanghai Hongqiao 18:32. 4.5 hrs. Book on Trip.com.

Dietary notes

  • Beijing is heavy on lamb, duck, and pork. Spicy options widely available.
  • Shanghai leans sweeter (Shanghainese cuisine uses sugar). Seafood dominant.
  • Vegetarian options exist but ask explicitly — stock-based broths are common even in "vegetable" dishes.
  • Dianping photos + pointing works well for ordering without language.

Beijing

3 – 9 Aug · 6 nights
3Mon

Arrival day easy

Check in · recover · first meal out

Flight lands, clear customs, get to the hotel. Most museums close Mondays — don't plan sights. Instead:

  • Walk the immediate neighbourhood, get your bearings
  • First dinner: Siji Minfu (四季民福) near Wangfujing — roast duck done properly, no frills, beloved on Xiaohongshu. Queue or book on Dianping.
  • Download and set up Dianping, Amap, and confirm Alipay is working
4Tue

Oriental Plaza & Wangfujing easy

Shopping · street food · city centre ease
  • Oriental Plaza (东方广场) — high-end mall anchoring Wangfujing; good for browsing, AC, and coffee
  • Wangfujing Snack Street — chaotic and fun, street snacks, scorpions on sticks (you don't have to eat them)
  • Afternoon: Beijing APM or nearby Shin Kong Place if you want more retail
  • Evening: ramen or hand-pulled noodles in the area — ask Dianping for what's rated nearby
5Wed

Forbidden City & Tiananmen active

Pre-book tickets · enter at Meridian Gate (south)
  • Tiananmen Square (pre-booked, free) — arrive early before the heat
  • Forbidden City (故宫) — 2–3 hrs minimum; focus on the central axis and Imperial Garden. Audio guide in English available.
  • Midday rest — exit north, find lunch in Jingshan Park area
  • Afternoon: Jingshan Park (景山公园) — the hill directly behind the Forbidden City gives the iconic aerial view. ¥2 entry.
  • Evening: hutong dinner — Gulou area, walk Nanluoguxiang and pick a spot on Dianping
6Thu

Shichahai Lakes & Pure Teahouse easy

Hutong wander · lake scenery · tea ceremony
  • Morning: walk Shichahai (什刹海) — the lakes and willow-lined lanes that give old Beijing its feel
  • Yandai Xiejie (烟袋斜街) — small hutong street with vintage shops and cafés
  • Afternoon: Pure Lotus Teahouse (净莲漫茶) or another of the lakeside teahouses — sit, slow down, order a proper gaiwan service
  • Evening: hotpot in the area. Haidilao is the reliable crowd-pleaser; local Sichuan places on Dianping for more adventurous options.
7Fri

Great Wall at Mutianyu active

Full day · cable car up · toboggan optional
  • Leave by 07:30 to beat crowds and heat. Shared van (book via hotel or Klook ~¥300pp return) or Didi + public bus.
  • Mutianyu (慕田峪) — the best-preserved, least crowded stretch with forest backdrop. Cable car up, toboggan down (¥100, genuinely fun).
  • Allow 3–4 hrs on the wall. Bring water, sunscreen, and sturdy shoes.
  • Back in Beijing by 17:00. Rest, then light evening dinner — Baodu Feng (爆肚冯) near Gulou for offal hot pot, or noodles.
8Sat

Lama Temple, Guozijian & Fuding Coffee easy

Culture · Michelin lunch · rooftop coffee
  • Morning: Yonghe Gong (雍和宫 Lama Temple) — Beijing's most atmospheric functioning temple. Incense, monks, incredible interiors. Go early before tour groups.
  • Walk Guozijian Street (国子监街) — the old Imperial College street, lined with scholar trees and small book/antique shops
  • Lunch: 翰林书院 Hanlin Shuyuan — Michelin-recommended, housed in a restored courtyard near the temple. Reserve on Dianping.
  • Coffee: 福顶咖啡 Fuding Coffee — rooftop overlooking the Lama Temple. Wildly photogenic; popular on Xiaohongshu.
  • Evening: free. Pack for tomorrow's train.
9Sun

🚄 Beijing → Shanghai

Check out in the morning. Light lunch near hotel. Didi to Beijing South Station (北京南站) — allow 60 min from central Beijing. Train departs 14:00, arrives Shanghai Hongqiao 18:32. Check in, settle, dinner near the hotel. Rest.

Shanghai

9 – 16 Aug · 7 nights
9Sun

Arrive Shanghai easy

Check in · neighbourhood explore

Arrive Hongqiao 18:32. Metro Line 2 or 10 to your hotel (or Didi). Check in, freshen up. Dinner near the hotel — keep it simple tonight. Jia Jia Tang Bao (佳家汤包) for soup dumplings if there's one nearby, or a local ramen spot on Dianping.

10Mon

French Concession shopping easy

Wukang Road · boutiques · café crawl
  • Wukang Road (武康路) — the most photographed street in Shanghai; plane trees, Art Deco villas, independent boutiques. Best mid-morning before crowds.
  • Anfu Road and Changle Road — vintage shops, designer coffee, the kind of places that go viral on Xiaohongshu
  • Xintiandi (新天地) — renovated Shikumen houses, upscale dining and shopping. Great for afternoon coffee and people-watching.
  • Evening: French Concession dinner. Jesse Restaurant (吉士酒家) for classic Shanghainese — red-braised pork, drunken chicken. Book ahead on Dianping.
11Tue

Nanjing Road, the Bund & Old Town easy

Iconic waterfront · old city · street food
  • Morning: Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street (南京路步行街) — the main shopping drag. Crowded but worth seeing once; pick up local snacks.
  • The Bund (外滩) — best in the morning before the heat peaks. The colonial-era riverside façade with Pudong skyline behind it. Walk the full promenade.
  • Lunch: Yang's Fry-Dumpling (小杨生煎) near People's Square — pan-fried dumplings, famously crispy bottoms. Queue is part of the experience.
  • Afternoon: Yu Garden & Old Town (豫园) — classical Ming garden, surrounding bazaar. Touristy but the garden itself is genuinely beautiful.
  • Evening: Bund rooftop bar for skyline views — Bar Rouge or New Heights at Three on the Bund.
12Wed

Suzhou day trip active

Classical gardens · canals · day return
  • Bullet train from Shanghai Hongqiao or Shanghai Railway Station — 25–35 min. First train ~07:00, last return ~21:00. Book the night before on Trip.com.
  • Humble Administrator's Garden (拙政园) — the largest and most celebrated classical garden in China. Pre-book timed entry.
  • Tiger Hill (虎丘) — leaning pagoda, rock gardens, less polished than the formal gardens but more atmospheric. Book timed entry.
  • Lunch: Suzhou noodles — braised duck or squirrel-shaped mandarin fish. Ask Dianping for what's rated near Tiger Hill.
  • Pingjiang Road (平江路) — the canal-lined old street; afternoon wander before the return train.
  • Back in Shanghai by 19:00. Light dinner.
13Thu

West Bund art corridor easy

Galleries · Long Museum · riverside walk
  • West Bund (西岸) — Shanghai's answer to London's Southbank. A string of art institutions along the Huangpu river.
  • Long Museum West Bund (龙美术馆) — impressive contemporary Chinese art collection in a brutalist former coal facility. Check current exhibition before visiting.
  • Yuz Museum — international contemporary art, design focus.
  • Tank Shanghai (油罐艺术中心) — five converted oil tanks; art, a park, and great coffee. Very Instagrammable.
  • Afternoon: walk the West Bund waterfront. Much quieter than the Bund, great views.
  • Evening: dinner in the Jing'an area — Jing'an Kerry Centre area has good options across price points.
14Fri

Pudong: Shanghai Museum East & skyline active

New museum · tower views · Lujiazui
  • Shanghai Museum East (上海博物馆东馆) — the vast new branch in Pudong, opened 2024. Ancient Chinese art and artefacts. Timed entry via WeChat mini-programme. Closed Mondays.
  • Afternoon: Lujiazui skyline — the cluster of towers. Go up Shanghai Tower (上海中心) observation deck (135F, 632m) for the city view. Book online to avoid queues.
  • Walk along the Pudong waterfront for the reverse Bund view — best at dusk.
  • Evening: dinner in Pudong or back in Puxi (Bund side). Ultraviolet by Paul Pairet if you booked months ago; otherwise Hakkasan or any of the Bund-adjacent restaurants.
15Sat

Slow last day (Zhujiajiao optional) easy

Water town · or rest · final dinner
  • Option A – Zhujiajiao (朱家角): the most accessible water town from central Shanghai (~1 hr by bus or Didi). Stone bridges, canals, boat rides. Works as a half-day; leave by 09:00, back by 14:00.
  • Option B – Rest day: revisit your favourite neighbourhood, pick up any last gifts, spa afternoon.
  • Final dinner: make it a good one. Ultraviolet (if booked), or a Shanghainese feast — try Fuli (福里) or The Lost Heaven for Yunnan cuisine as a different note to end on.
16Sun

✈ Fly home to London

Check out, Didi or Metro to Pudong (PVG) or Hongqiao (SHA) — confirm which airport your flight uses. Allow 2 hrs for international check-in. Safe flight.

Beijing spots

food & places
Siji Minfu 四季民福 Peking Duck Near Wangfujing / Forbidden City The Xiaohongshu-approved roast duck spot. Proper duck, proper price, no tourist theatre. Book on Dianping or queue.
Baodu Feng 爆肚冯 Traditional Gulou area Famous for lamb offal hot pot — tripe, stomach, the works. Old Beijing institution. Not for the faint-hearted but genuinely local.
Hanlin Shuyuan 翰林书院 Michelin Near Lama Temple Michelin-recommended courtyard restaurant near Yonghe Gong. Refined Beijing cuisine in a restored setting. Book ahead.
Fuding Coffee 福顶咖啡 Café Overlooking Lama Temple Rooftop café with unobstructed views over Yonghe Gong. Very popular on Xiaohongshu. Go mid-morning on a weekday.
Yonghe Gong 雍和宫 Temple Dongcheng District Beijing's most atmospheric working Tibetan Buddhist temple. Five halls, incense courtyards, 18m sandalwood Buddha. Go early.
Mutianyu Great Wall 慕田峪 Day trip ~90 min from central Beijing Best-preserved section, forest setting, far fewer crowds than Badaling. Cable car up, optional toboggan down.
Shichahai 什刹海 Lakes Xicheng District Three interconnected lakes ringed by willows and old hutong lanes. Best in the morning. Hire a paddle boat or just walk.
Wudaoying Hutong 五道营胡同 Shopping Near Lama Temple The cooler, less touristy hutong strip. Independent cafés, vintage clothing, ceramics. Better than Nanluoguxiang for actual shopping.
Skip the hype — Beijing: Quanjude duck (overrated, tourist pricing, mediocre quality vs Siji Minfu). Nanluoguxiang for shopping (too commercial; Wudaoying is better).

Shanghai spots

food & places
Jia Jia Tang Bao 佳家汤包 Soup Dumplings Multiple locations The soup dumpling (xiaolongbao) spot that locals and Dianping users rate above Din Tai Fung. Thin skin, intense broth. Always a queue — go early.
Yang's Fry-Dumpling 小杨生煎 Pan-fried Near People's Square Shanghai's most famous shengjian bao. Crispy base, juicy pork, broth burst. Order at the window, eat standing up. Queue is the norm.
Jesse Restaurant 吉士酒家 Shanghainese French Concession Classic Shanghainese cooking — red-braised pork, drunken chicken, smoked fish. A local institution, not a tourist trap. Book on Dianping.
Wukang Road 武康路 Street French Concession The most photographed street in Shanghai. Art Deco villas, plane trees, independent boutiques and cafés. Peak Xiaohongshu territory.
West Bund Art Corridor 西岸 Galleries Xuhui Riverside Long Museum, Yuz Museum, Tank Shanghai — all within walking distance along the Huangpu river. A full afternoon's worth of contemporary art.
Tank Shanghai 油罐艺术中心 Art Park West Bund Five converted oil tanks turned gallery/park/café. Architecturally striking, great coffee, popular with locals on weekends.
Shanghai Museum East 上海博物馆东馆 Museum Pudong, Huamu Road The stunning new Pudong branch (opened 2024). Ancient Chinese bronzes, ceramics, jades. Timed entry required. Closed Mondays.
Suzhou day trip Day trip 25–35 min by bullet train Humble Administrator's Garden + Tiger Hill + Pingjiang Road canal walk. Pre-book garden timed entries. Easy full-day return.
Skip the hype — Shanghai: The 13DE MARZO bear drink (30-min queue for an average matcha; go elsewhere). Din Tai Fung (fine but overpriced and always packed; Jia Jia is better and cheaper). The Bund at midday in August (brutal heat; go morning or evening).
