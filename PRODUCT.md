# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Anthony and his partner — two people planning a two-week trip to Beijing and Shanghai in August 2026. The audience is exactly two; this is not a public guide, a travel blog, or a product for strangers. They know the context and are using this as a personal reference: to feel organised, excited, and prepared.

## Product Purpose

A private editorial travel guide for one specific trip. It exists to hold all the planning — itinerary, logistics, shopping picks, spots, maps — in one place, designed to be re-read in the weeks before the trip and used on the ground. Success means the page makes the trip feel vivid and real before it happens, and useful and navigable during it.

## Brand Personality

Intimate, curated, warm. The register is a private exhibition catalogue made for someone you love — not a travel agency brochure, not a tech product, not a public-facing guide. It should feel like Anthony made it with care: specific recommendations, considered prose, something you'd actually want to read. Three words: **intimate · curated · warm**.

## Anti-references

- Generic travel sites (Lonely Planet, TripAdvisor, Booking.com) — mass-market, impersonal, optimised for strangers
- Tourist brochures — promotional, breathless, hollow
- Tech-style travel apps (Airbnb Experiences aesthetic, startup polish) — too clean, too product-brained, no warmth
- SaaS design language of any kind — this is not a dashboard, not a tool, not a workflow

## Design Principles

1. **Made for two, not for broadcast.** Every decision should feel personal — not polished for an audience that doesn't exist. The specificity of the recommendations (the 07:30 departure, the ¥2 hill entry, the toboggan down Mutianyu) is the personality.

2. **Tactility over decoration.** The design should feel like materials — paper you can almost touch, ink you can almost smell, stone underfoot. Not flat and digital. Texture and weight come from restraint and density, not from ornament layered on ornament.

3. **Quiet confidence.** The design has already reached for elegance; the aspiration is to be more spare and assured. Decoration that doesn't earn its place should be cut. The refined moments (the bronze rule ornament, the chop circles, the transit strip) work because they're chosen; that principle should extend everywhere.

4. **Cinematic pacing.** The day-by-day structure is a narrative with real dramatic beats: arriving in Beijing, the Wall at dawn, stepping off the train into Shanghai, the final Bund skyline at dusk. The design should honor that rhythm — those moments should land with more emotional weight than an ordinary day-entry.

5. **Warmth in the particular.** Personality comes from the specific, not from visual decoration. The copy already does this; the design should amplify it — not compete with it, and not muffle it with excessive chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA** as the baseline target: body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast against background, large text ≥ 3:1
- **prefers-reduced-motion** already implemented; preserve and extend as new motion is added
- All interactive elements must have visible focus states (`:focus-visible` already wired to `--bj` outline)
